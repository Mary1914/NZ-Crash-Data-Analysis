NZ Crash Data Analysis

This project analyses official New Zealand road crash data to identify patterns related to time, weather, region, and road type. The goal is to support evidence-based road safety decisions.

  • Crash & injury trends by day, hour, and region
  • Impact of weather conditions on crash severity
  • Fatal crash patterns across road categories
View Dashboards ↓

Key Insights (Summary)

  • Weekdays show higher injury counts compared to weekends
  • Most crashes occur during daytime and evening peak hours
  • Fine weather still accounts for the majority of crashes, indicating volume risk
  • Urban arterial and major roads contribute heavily to total crash counts
  • Fatal crashes vary significantly by region and year

Dashboard 1: Injuries by Day, Weather, and Severity

Key insight: Injury counts are highest on weekdays, particularly under fine weather conditions. This suggests commuter traffic during normal working days is a major contributor to road injuries, rather than adverse weather alone.

Dashboard 2: Crash Patterns by Time and Road Category

Key insight: Crash volumes peak during morning and evening hours, aligning with typical rush-hour periods. Arterial and urban roads show consistently higher crash counts, highlighting traffic density and road usage as key risk factors.

Dashboard 3: Fatal Crash Trends by Region (1999–2024)

Key insight: Fatal crash trends vary significantly by region, with some regions showing persistently higher fatal counts over time. This indicates the need for region-specific road safety interventions rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.
