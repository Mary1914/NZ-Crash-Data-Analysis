Key Insights

1) Regional crash concentration

  • Crash counts are not evenly distributed — a small number of regions contribute a large share.
  • This indicates clear opportunities for targeted enforcement and infrastructure upgrades.

2) Weather conditions matter

  • Crash trends change under poor weather conditions (e.g., wet or low-visibility periods).
  • This suggests road condition messaging and winter/seasonal readiness campaigns can reduce risk.

3) Time-of-day / day-of-week patterns

  • Crash patterns vary across time periods, suggesting predictable high-risk windows.
  • Targeting these windows helps optimise police patrols and public awareness campaigns.

Recommendations

  • Target high-crash regions: prioritise engineering improvements (lighting, signage, barriers) and enforcement in top regions.
  • Weather-triggered safety actions: activate alerts and reduce speed messaging during high-risk weather conditions.
  • Time-based scheduling: align patrols, awareness campaigns, and driver messaging with peak risk time periods.
  • Continuous monitoring: build monthly/quarterly dashboards to track whether actions reduce crash rates.
